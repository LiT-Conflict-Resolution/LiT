Conflict Resolution in Warehouse Robotics

Goutham Muralikrishnan · Noah Steinkrüger · Vinay Orsu
University of Technology Nuremberg (UTN), Department CSAI — Nuremberg, Germany

Abstract

We present a fully decentralized conflict detection and resolution module that runs onboard each robot. Conflicts are triggered from LiDAR/odometry/commanded velocity cues, and resolved via a lightweight QP safety filter with a liveness (yielding) constraint to break deadlocks.

Key contributions

  • Local LiDAR-based conflict triggering (no global coordination required)
  • QP velocity filter with barrier constraints for collision avoidance
  • Liveness constraint (yielding) to break symmetric deadlocks
  • Integration-friendly with standard navigation stacks (e.g., Nav2)
